Image Analysis

Here are some of various vision projects that I contributed over the years.

Image-guided tumor tracking for radiation therapy:

Collaborated with researchers in radiation oncology department of University of Maryland School of Medicine todevelop a robust real time tumor tracking model that incorporates information and labels from 4DCT on kV X-rayvideos for treatment-day specific tumor motion models. Formulated a novel edge preserving ray casting methodfor DRR formation and a non-parametric registration method to register 3D CT images to 2D kV X-ray images. Developed techniques can be used for robust real time lung tumor tracking to increases the accuracy of radiationtherapy by increasing the radiation dose given to tumor while limiting the exposure of nearby critical healthy tissues.

Image segmentation and analysis of MR sequences:

Measurement of brain iron distribution

Investigated the spatial distribution of iron accumulation in globus pallidus (GP) in patients with Hallevorden-Spatz Syndrome (HSS) also known as the pantothenate kinase-associated neurodegeneration (PKAN). Appliedphase-unwrapping, filtering, and segmentation techniques to obtain quantitative measures for the iron accumulationin the brain from Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) . Obtained results demonstrated higher sensitivity of the phase measurements for quantitative assessment of iron concentration compared to the relaxation rate measurements (R1,R2, and R2*).

T1 Polarity restoration

Developed robust regression algorithms to restore the signal polarity in IR sequence. By restoring the signal polarity in IR sequence, it is possible to use the whole dynamic range of the data and improve reliability of T1 relaxation time fits.
